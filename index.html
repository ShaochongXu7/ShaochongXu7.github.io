Biography

I am Shaochong Xu, a second-year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Civil and Systems Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, advised by Prof. Lauren Gardner. Under Prof. Gardner's supervision, I collaborate closely within our research group and with Prof. Hongru Du.

My research interests include computational epidemiology, data-driven decision-making, behavior modeling, and the intersection of systems engineering and public health.

Before joining Johns Hopkins for my Ph.D., I earned a M.S. in Systems Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, and a B.S. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen.

In my free time, I enjoy road trips, exploring good food, and listening to music.

Research Interests

Computational Epidemiology

  • Real-time surveillance and forecasting: Building data-driven models to track and forecast infectious disease dynamics using heterogeneous data streams (e.g., cases, mobility, digital traces).
  • Early warning and situational awareness: Developing robust indicators and evaluation pipelines to detect emerging outbreaks and improve operational decision support.

Behavior Modeling and Information Effects

  • Vaccination decision-making: Modeling how risk perception, beliefs, and social influence shape individual and population-level vaccination behavior.
  • Media and LLM-era information environments: Studying how online content and large language models may shift health information-seeking behavior and downstream public health outcomes.

Systems Engineering for Public Health

  • Data-driven decision-making: Designing modeling and optimization frameworks that translate data into actionable public health strategies.
  • Model interpretability and robustness: Improving transparency, uncertainty quantification to support reliable policy insights.
